Freaky Forms

Answer: FLYGON
by Kaley Brauer

Each pair of creatures represents a Pokemon from the first game, in an art style which suggests a certain type. Within pokemon, there is a canonical match up being types, where one type has an advantage over another, i.e. fire beats ice.

The matchups are given below, with the advantage type bolded:

Fire vs Grass
Fighting vs Ice
Electric vs Flying
Grass vs Poison
Fighting vs Flying
Dragon vs Dragon

In the case of dragon, it ends up being strong against itself. This clues us to the fact that what matters is the winning type, and not say left/right winner. Another useful piece of information is the pokedex number of each pokemon.

This number can be used to index into the winning type, and extract the answer reading down the columns:

PokemonPokedex #Winning TypeExtracted Letter
Bulbasaur1FIREF
Ivysaur2ELECTRICL
Venasaur3FLYINGY
Wartortle8FIGHTINGG
Charmeleon5POISONO
Charizard6DRAGONN

This gives us our final answer: FLYGON (which is itself a pokemon from a later generation)
